MEET THE POLYBIOME PROJECT

One in every 33 cancers is adscribed to processed or red meat.

In an unprecedented statement, the World Health Organitzation announced in October 2015 their concern in reference to recent studies by the International Agency for Research on Cancer (IARC) which related processed and red meat intake with colorectal cancer.

The studies found that 21% of bowel cancers are caused by processed or red meat, which acounts for a 3% of the total world cancers. In the Carcinogenic Groups classification processed meats rank now in the first risk group, and red meats in the second.

Polyamines are the main responsibles of the meat cancerigenous potential.

These small molecules are involved in processes such as cell proliferation, and are, in small quantities, essential for our bodies. However, if there is a desequilibrium in their concentration, for example, due to continuous meat intake, polyamines can upregulate proliferation, increasing cancer risk and becoming perjudicial for our health.

Once absorbed in the lower intestine, polyamines enter the blod floow and spread across the body, where efficient targeting and control is difficult. Therefore, it is nescesary an approach that eliminates them before they are absorbed.

Polybiome efectively controls polyamine levels by targeting them in the absorbing phase.

Polybiome is a probiotic consisting on bacteria which degrade the poliamines in the intestinal tract, reducing the external intake and thus minimizing the risk factor of cancer.
