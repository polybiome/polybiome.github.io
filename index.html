MEET THE POLYBIOME PROJECT

One in every 33 cancers is ascribed to processed or red meat.

In an unprecedented statement, the World Health Organization announced in October 2015 their concern in reference to recent studies by the International Agency for Research on Cancer (IARC) which related processed and red meat intake with colorectal cancer.

The studies found that 21% of bowel cancers are caused by processed or red meat, which accounts for a 3% of the total world cancers. In the Carcinogenic Groups classification processed meat rank now in the first risk group and red meat in the second.

Polyamines are primarily responsible for the potential carcinogenicity of meat.

These small molecules are involved in processes such as cell proliferation and are, in small quantities, essential for our bodies. However, if there is a imbalance in their concentration, for example, due to continuous meat intake, polyamines can upregulate proliferation, increasing cancer risk and becoming harmful for our health.

Once absorbed in the lower intestine, polyamines enter the blood flow and spread across the body, where efficient targeting and control is difficult. Therefore, it is necessary an approach that eliminates them before they are absorbed.

Polybiome aims to effectively reduce polyamine levels by targeting them in the absorbing phase.

Polybiome is a probiotic consisting on bacteria which degrade the poliamines in the intestinal tract, reducing the external intake and thus minimizing the risk factor of cancer.
